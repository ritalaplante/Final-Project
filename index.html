Highlights and Overview

This section explores patterns in gender at the TATE. This section aims to explore the prevelance of female artists within the TATE collection and the time periods during which these female artists created the majority of their artwork.

Gender Breakdown at the TATE

Like most art collections around the world, a majority of the TATE collection is composed of male artists. The lack of female representation means it's important for us to learn more about these artists. For instance, we can ask the following questions:

  • When did they create their work?
  • What mediums did they use?
  • When did they live and where did they live?

When did these artists create their work? When was their art aquired by the gallery?

Most female artists of the TATE collection were not creating art until the 20th century. Simillarly, TATE galleries were not collecting art created by female artists until the 20th century. Women were largely excluded from the art scene for many centuries, and even today their work often is under-collected. Notice:

  • Throughout the 18th and 19th centuries there is very little change in art created and collected. Art created by female artists during these periods was very rare.
